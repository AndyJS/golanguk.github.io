Speakers

  • Damian Gryski

    Booking.com @dgryski
    Damian Gryski is a software developer working on infrastructure at Booking.com. In his free time he fills his GitHub account with implementations of interesting computer science papers and has been using Go long enough to remember Makefiles and os.Error. Damian lives in Amsterdam with his family.

  • Mat Ryer

    GrayMeta @matryer
    Mat started playing with Go while it was still in BETA, and has used it ever since. In January 2015, he published Go Programming Blueprints in which readers build several real-world Go programs while exploring simple but powerful patterns and techniques. An active open-sourcer Mat has founded and contributed to many popular packages, and is today using Go to build a range of high-performance data services and web apps.

  • Matt Heath

    Mondo @mattheath
    Matt Heath is an engineer at Mondo, a new kind of digital bank. Having previously worked as the Technical Lead of Hailo's global platform, Matt has an unhealthy obsession for scaling fault tolerant, high volume, distributed systems, and spends his spare time working on open source projects such as Phosphor, a distributed tracing system.

  • Evan Huus

    Shopify @eapache
    Evan Huus is a developer by day and a musician by night. He works on a variety of different projects at Shopify, and has been fortunate to spend a substantial amount of his time writing Go. On the side, he is a core developer for Wireshark and spends far too much time tweaking his vimrc. His instrument of choice is the euphonium.

  • Peter Bourgon

    Weave @peterbourgon
    Peter Bourgon is a distributed systems engineer who has seen things. He's been a Go programmer and advocate since day 1, and has written Go in production for the better part of 4 years. Peter has worked on infrastructure at Bloomberg, SoundCloud, and now works on software-defined networking and monitoring at Weaveworks.

  • William Kennedy

    Ardan Studio @goinggodotnet
    William Kennedy is a managing partner at Ardan Studio in Miami, Florida, a mobile, web, and systems development company. He is also a co-author of the book Go in Action, the author of the blog GoingGo.Net, and the organizer of the Go meetup in Miami. Bill is focused on Go education through his training company Ardan Labs. He can often be found talking at conferences and giving workshops both locally and over hangouts. He always finds time to work with individuals and groups who want to take their Go knowledge, blogging, and coding skills to the next level.

  • Dean Elbaz

    Songkick @DeanElbaz
    Dean has been programming in Go since since version 1.0. Impressed at first with its clean, readable style and great open source source community, he took a particular interest in Go's internals. He is currently a Software Engineer at Songkick working on their micro-service architecture for selling concert tickets worldwide. In his free time, you'll find Dean attending or speaking at the London Go user group, playing video games, or drinking French Ros√© with his wife.

  • Andrew Gerrand

    Google @enneff
    Andrew Gerrand works on the Go Programming Language at Google Sydney. He has written dozens of articles about Go, and given many talks and workshops at conferences around the world. He is the co-author of A Tour of Go, and is the fourth most prolific contributor to the Go project. He is passionate about software quality, and believes Go is a unique tool for building reliable software at scale. Before Go and Google, Andrew wrote software for Internet companies.

  • Ben Darnell

    Cockroach Labs @bendarnell
    Ben Darnell is the CTO and co-founder of Cockroach Labs where he works on the distributed consensus protocols that underpin its transactional model. He started his career at Google and then went on to a series of startups where he saw firsthand the need for better scalable storage systems.

  • Eleanor McHugh

    Games With Brains @feyeleanor
    London-based hacker Ellie is co-designer of the Yoti digital identity system and the sometime writer of A Go Developer's Notebook. During the course of her career she's worked on mission critical systems ranging from avionics to banking security and digital trust arbitration. As a responsible parent Ellie enjoys polyhedral dice, home brewing and gothic music.

  • Francesc Campoy

    Google @francesc
    Francesc Campoy Flores is a Developer Advocate for Go and the Cloud at Google. He joined the Go team in 2012 and since then he has written some considerable didactic resources and traveled the world attending conferences, organizing live courses, and meeting fellow gophers. He joined Google in 2011 as a backend software engineer working mostly in C++ and Python, but it was with Go that he rediscovered how fun programming can be. You can find him on twitter as @francesc.

  • David Calavera

    Docker @calavera
    David Calavera is one of the core maintainers of Docker. In his spare time, he writes a newsletter called Container Weekly and grows tomatoes in his backyard. A long time ago he used to write code for GitHub and contribute to other programming languages, like JRuby.
